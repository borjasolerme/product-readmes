# Filmia: AI video production for creative teams, from brief to final cut in one workspace

We built Filmia because we were tired of the mess.

Video production tools have never been better and AI models are wild right now. But when we worked with creative teams (and ran our own projects), the same thing kept happening. Scripts lived in Google Docs. Storyboards in Figma or on someone's iPad. AI-generated assets came from five different tools with five different logins. Edits happened in Premiere or DaVinci. Reviews played out over email chains, Slack threads, and Vimeo links with timestamps buried in comments. By the time a video went from concept to delivery, the creative energy was gone. Replaced by project management fatigue.

We lived that pain firsthand. So we built the tool we wished existed.

Filmia is our AI video production platform for creative teams and agencies. It pulls the entire workflow (brief, storyboard, asset generation, editing, review) into one collaborative workspace. Instead of stitching together a Frankenstein stack, your team works in one place from first idea to final export.

## The real problem: tool fragmentation is killing creative teams

We asked dozens of creative directors what slowed their teams down. The answer was rarely "we need better AI." It was coordination. Moving assets between tools. Keeping everyone on the same version. Translating a Slack comment into an actionable edit. Waiting for renders. Re-exporting for the third time because someone found a typo.

The average creative team uses 8-12 tools in their video workflow. Each one does its job fine. But the gaps between them create friction that compounds with every project. Context switches burn creative energy. Format conversions introduce quality loss. Version control becomes a nightmare when assets live in multiple places.

AI has actually made this worse. Now you've got Midjourney for images, Runway for video gen, ElevenLabs for voiceovers, Kling for specific effects, and a dozen other specialized tools. Each with its own interface, pricing, and learning curve. The output quality is often impressive. But bouncing between all of them to produce one coherent video is exhausting. We know because we were doing exactly that.

Our answer: bring the AI models into the production workspace instead of sending creatives out to use them separately. Inside Filmia you can access Kling AI, Flux, Runway, Google VEO, Sora, Seedream, Minimax, ElevenLabs, Wan, Luma, Topaz, Clarity, and more. No separate accounts. No file transfers. No context switching. The assets you generate go directly into your project timeline.

If your team spends more time managing tools than making things, 👉 [request access to Filmia](https://www.filmia.ai) and see what an integrated AI production workflow actually feels like. We built this because we lived the fragmentation problem every day.

## What makes Filmia different from other video production tools

The video tool market is crowded. Most platforms fall into one of two buckets: creative tools (editors, asset generators) or project management tools (review platforms, approval workflows). We designed Filmia to bridge both.

The biggest decision we made early on was to cover the end-to-end workflow. Most AI video tools focus on generation. Prompt in, video out. Filmia covers the whole production process: briefing, storyboarding, AI asset generation, professional editing, team review, and final delivery. That changes how creative work gets organized, not just how fast individual tasks get done.

We invested heavily in the brand control system. You define your brand identity (visual style, tone, colors, typography, messaging guidelines) and the AI applies those rules across everything it generates. Scripts, images, final video outputs. All consistent. For agencies managing multiple brands, switching between clients doesn't mean reconfiguring every tool in your stack. We built this because we saw agencies waste hours manually enforcing brand guidelines on AI-generated content.

Collaboration is where our design philosophy really shows. Shareable review links let stakeholders comment with timestamps directly on the video. Team members reuse assets across projects through a shared library. Everyone works in the same workspace with real-time visibility into project status. No more "which version is the latest?" chaos.

We built security and compliance in from day one, not as an afterthought. End-to-end encryption, data encryption at rest, GDPR compliance, EU AI Act compliance. Being based in Milan, we take European regulation seriously. For enterprise clients and agencies handling sensitive brand assets, these aren't nice-to-haves. They're table stakes. Filmia handles them natively.

One thing we want to be clear about: Filmia isn't trying to be a beginner-friendly video maker. We built it for professional creative teams who know what they're doing and want AI to speed up their workflow, not replace their judgment. The interface assumes creative competence and gives you powerful tools without dumbing them down.

## Breaking down the features that actually matter

**Brief to storyboard**

Every project starts with a brief. You define objectives, target audience, messaging, and visual direction. We built Filmia to translate that into a structured storyboard. The AI suggests scene compositions, shot sequences, and visual treatments based on your brief. Your team makes all final decisions.

We made the storyboard a living blueprint connected to every downstream stage. When you move to production, each scene carries its context forward. The editor knows what each shot is supposed to achieve. Asset generation pulls from the established visual direction. This was intentional. We wanted every stage to talk to the next.

**Multi-model AI asset generation**

This is where the integration advantage gets real. Instead of hopping between Midjourney for images, Runway for video clips, and ElevenLabs for audio, you access all of them from within Filmia. Plus Kling AI, Flux, Google VEO, Sora, Seedream, Minimax, Wan, Luma, Topaz, Clarity, and others.

Generate an image with one model, turn it into a video clip with another, add a voiceover with a third, and composite them together in the editor. All without leaving the platform. We did the hard work of integrating these models so you don't have to manage the technical plumbing. You focus on creative choices instead of file format headaches.

**Professional video editing**

The built-in editor covers what you'd expect: timeline-based editing, multi-track audio, transitions, text overlays, color adjustments, and effects. We also added AI-powered tools like video upscaling, relighting, background removal, and lip-sync adjustment.

We built this for professionals. Experienced editors get the tools they expect, plus AI assistance for tasks that traditionally eat up time. Upscaling a clip, removing a background, adjusting lighting. These used to require specialized software and real patience. In Filmia, they're built-in functions.

**Brand-consistent generation**

Configure your brand guidelines once. Every AI-generated asset automatically follows them. Scripts adopt your brand's tone of voice. Images use your color palette and visual style. This consistency applies across all AI models on the platform, so you get a coherent look regardless of which model generates each asset.

For agencies managing multiple brands, this is a big deal. We designed it so you can switch to a different client's brand profile, and all generation immediately follows that client's identity. No manual adjustment. No style guides that get ignored. No off-brand assets that need revision.

**Collaborative review**

Share review links with clients or stakeholders who can comment directly on the video with timestamps. Comments are visible to the entire team, so feedback doesn't get lost in email. Approvals, revision requests, and final sign-offs happen in one place with a clear record.

We built this to replace the typical flow of exporting a draft, uploading to a review platform, collecting feedback in a separate channel, and translating that into edits. In Filmia, feedback lives alongside the edit. Acting on it is immediate.

**Enterprise support**

For enterprise clients, we provide weekly working sessions and hands-on AI training. Not generic customer support. Our team actually works with you to integrate the platform into your existing production workflows. We know that for teams transitioning from traditional to AI-augmented production, guided onboarding makes a real difference in how quickly people get comfortable. We've seen it firsthand.

Organizations that want fully automated, hands-off video generation. We designed Filmia for creative teams that want AI to accelerate their work, not replace it. If you want prompt in, finished video out, no humans involved, Filmia has more human touchpoints than you want.

Companies with very limited budgets. Filmia is a professional production platform with enterprise-grade features. We positioned it for teams where video production is a core business function. If that's not you yet, that's okay.

We respect the tools in this space. Here's how we see ourselves relative to them and why we think we're different.

These platforms make video creation accessible with templates and simple editors. They're designed for non-professional creators who need serviceable content quickly. **Filmia vs. Individual AI Tool Stacks**

The real alternative to Filmia isn't a single competitor. It's the collection of 8-12 tools most teams currently use. Midjourney + Runway + ElevenLabs + Premiere + Frame.io + Google Drive + Slack. We built Filmia to replace that fragmented stack with an integrated platform. The honest trade-off: you give up best-in-class capability from each specialized tool in exchange for a unified workflow. Whether that works for you depends on how much the fragmentation is actually costing you. For the teams we've talked to, it's costing a lot more than they realized.

## Practical tips for getting started

These come from watching teams onboard and from our own production work. The teams that get value fastest tend to follow this playbook.

**Set up brand profiles before your first project**

Spend time configuring thorough brand guidelines in Filmia before starting production work. AI-generated assets get noticeably better when the system has clear brand parameters to work within. We've seen this over and over. This upfront work pays off across every future project.

**Start with a small, real project**

Don't test Filmia with a hypothetical project. Use a real upcoming deliverable so you can honestly compare it to your current workflow. The integration advantages become most obvious when you're working under real deadlines with real stakeholders.

**Get the whole team involved early**

The collaboration features only matter when the team actually uses them. Get editors, designers, copywriters, and reviewers into the platform from the start. The workflow benefits grow as more of the work happens in the same workspace.

**Experiment with multiple AI models**

Don't default to one AI model for everything. We give you access to many, and each has strengths for different types of content. Try different models for different scene types. Build an intuition for which one works best for what.

**Use review links for client feedback**

Replace email-based review with Filmia's shareable review links. Timestamped comments on the actual video are clearer, more actionable, and create a better record than paragraphs of text describing what the client wants changed.
